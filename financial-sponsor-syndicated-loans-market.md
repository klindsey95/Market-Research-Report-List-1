<p><p>In the financial sponsor and syndicated loans market, there are three primary deal types. An underwritten deal involves lead banks committing fully to the loan, assuming risk by underwriting the entire amount before syndicating it. A club deal consists of a small group of banks or lenders that share the loan risk and work collaboratively. In a best-efforts syndication deal, the lead bank commits to selling the loan but does not guarantee the total amount, sharing the risk with the borrower.</p></p>
