<p><p>Spinal Interbody Fusion is a surgical procedure designed to alleviate pain and restore stability in the spine by fusing two or more vertebrae together. This technique involves inserting a bone graft or interbody spacer into the disc space between the vertebrae, promoting bone growth and ultimately stabilizing the spine. Often utilized to treat conditions such as degenerative disc disease, spinal stenosis, and spondylolisthesis, this procedure aims to provide significant relief and improve patients' quality of life.</p><p>The Spinal Interbody Fusion Market is experiencing substantial growth, driven by the increasing prevalence of spinal disorders, technological advancements in surgical techniques, and a rising aging population.
