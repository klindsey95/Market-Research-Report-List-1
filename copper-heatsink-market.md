<p><ul><li>Passive Heat Sink</li><li>Active Heat Sink</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The copper heatsink market comprises two primary types: passive and active heat sinks. Passive heat sinks rely on natural convection and thermal conduction to dissipate heat, utilizing their large surface area for effective cooling without any mechanical components. In contrast, active heat sinks incorporate fans or other cooling mechanisms to enhance airflow and improve heat dissipation, making them suitable for high-performance applications. Both types leverage copper's superior thermal conductivity, ensuring efficient temperature management in electronic devices.</p></p>
