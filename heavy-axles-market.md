<p><p>The Heavy Axles market comprises Drive Axles and Non-Driven Axles. Drive Axles are integral to power transmission, transferring engine torque to wheels, commonly used in trucks, buses, and heavy-duty vehicles. Non-Driven Axles, also known as dead axles, support vehicle weight and enhance stability without power transfer, often found in trailers or multi-axle setups. Both types cater to heavy-duty applications, ensuring load-bearing capacity, durability, and performance, with Drive Axles focusing on propulsion and Non-Driven Axles emphasizing structural support and balance.</p></p>
