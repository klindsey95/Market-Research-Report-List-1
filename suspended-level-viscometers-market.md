<p><ul><li>Type I</li><li>Type II</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Suspended-level viscometers are devices designed to measure the viscosity of fluids based on the motion of objects suspended in the fluid. In the market, Type I viscometers often utilize rotational methods, where a rotating element is immersed in the fluid, while Type II viscometers commonly rely on falling ball or capillary methods. Both types serve specific applications, with Type I suitable for high-viscosity materials and Type II ideal for low-viscosity fluids, catering to distinct industry needs and measurement requirements.</p></p>
