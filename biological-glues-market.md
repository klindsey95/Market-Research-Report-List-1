<p><p>The biological glues market comprises various types including proteins, carbohydrates, glycoproteins, mucopolysaccharides, and others. Proteins, such as collagen and gelatin, are known for their strong adhesive properties in medical and industrial applications. Carbohydrates, like chitosan, provide biocompatibility and are used in wound healing. Glycoproteins combine protein and sugar components, facilitating cell adhesion. Mucopolysaccharides, such as hyaluronic acid, exhibit excellent moisture retention and are used in cosmetics and medicine. Other materials include bioadhesives derived from natural sources, catering to diverse applications.</p></p>
