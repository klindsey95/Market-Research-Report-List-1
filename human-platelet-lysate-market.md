<p><p>The Human Platelet Lysate (HPL) market is distinguished by two primary types: Heparin and Heparin-Free. Heparinized HPL contains heparin, an anticoagulant that helps prevent clotting, making it suitable for applications that require prolonged cell culture and enhanced cellular growth. In contrast, Heparin-Free HPL is designed for situations where heparin-induced complications are a concern, particularly in cell therapies and regenerative medicine. Both types offer unique benefits tailored to specific research and clinical applications, influencing their adoption in various sectors.</p></p>
