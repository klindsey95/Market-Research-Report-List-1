<p><p>The spinal osteosynthesis units market is segmented into three primary types: posterior, anterior, and lateral. </p><p>Posterior approaches involve accessing the spine from the back, ideal for conditions like degenerative disc disease. Anterior methods access the spine through the front, often used for correcting deformities and accessing the thoracic spine. Lateral approaches, entering from the side, are beneficial for minimally invasive surgeries, particularly in the lumbar region. Each approach offers unique advantages depending on the surgical goals and patient needs.</p></p>
