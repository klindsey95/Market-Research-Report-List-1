<p><p>The automotive leather upholstery market is primarily divided into two types: genuine leather and synthetic leather. Genuine leather, made from animal hide, is prized for its durability, luxury feel, and aging characteristics, often appealing to high-end automotive segments. In contrast, synthetic leather, typically crafted from polyurethane (PU) or polyvinyl chloride (PVC), offers a cost-effective, versatile alternative that mimics the appearance of real leather while being lighter and more resistant to stains and moisture, catering to a broader consumer base.</p></p>
