<p><p>The rice cracker market is divided into two primary types: non-glutinous rice base and glutinous rice base. Non-glutinous rice crackers, made from regular rice, typically have a crisp texture and are often low in calories, appealing to health-conscious consumers. In contrast, glutinous rice crackers, crafted from sticky rice, provide a chewy, flexible texture and are popular in Asian cuisines, often featuring unique flavors. Both types cater to diverse consumer preferences, serving as snacks or accompaniments to meals.</p></p>
