<p><p>The antiarrhythmic drugs market is categorized into two primary types: oral antiarrhythmic and intravenous antiarrhythmic medications. Oral antiarrhythmics are typically prescribed for long-term management of arrhythmias and are taken in pill form, offering convenience and ease of use for patients. In contrast, intravenous antiarrhythmics are administered directly into the bloodstream, allowing for rapid onset of action, often used in emergency settings to quickly stabilize heart rhythm. Both types play crucial roles in managing various cardiac conditions.</p></p>
