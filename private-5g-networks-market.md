<p><p>Private 5G networks can be categorized into two main types based on frequency: Sub-6 GHz and mmWave. Sub-6 GHz networks offer broader coverage and better penetration, making them suitable for large facilities and rural areas. They provide reliable connectivity for various applications, including IoT and automation. In contrast, mmWave networks operate at higher frequencies, delivering faster speeds and lower latency but with limited range. These are ideal for high-density environments like stadiums or smart factories, requiring robust data transfer capabilities.</p></p>
